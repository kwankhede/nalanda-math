A percentage is a way to express a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" comes from Latin "per centum" meaning "per hundred".

Percentages are everywhere in daily life ‚Äî discounts in shops, exam scores, tax rates, interest rates, statistics in news, and growth rates in business. Understanding percentages is essential for making informed decisions!

This chapter covers converting between fractions, decimals, and percentages; calculating percentage increase/decrease; profit and loss; simple and compound interest; and real-world applications ‚Äî with 100+ practice problems to master these concepts.

Finding Percentages | ‡§ü‡§ï‡•ç‡§ï‡•á‡§µ‡§æ‡§∞‡•Ä ‡§∂‡•ã‡§ß‡§£‡•á

Formula: Finding x% of a number

x% of N = (x/100) √ó N

Example 1: Find 20% of 500

= (20/100) √ó 500 = 0.2 √ó 500 = 100

Example 2: Find 15% of 80

= (15/100) √ó 80 = 0.15 √ó 80 = 12

Formula: What percentage is A of B?

Percentage = (A/B) √ó 100%

Example 1: What % is 30 of 150?

= (30/150) √ó 100% = (1/5) √ó 100% = 20%

Example 2: What % is 45 of 60?

= (45/60) √ó 100% = (3/4) √ó 100% = 75%

Formula: Find the whole when percentage is known

Whole = (Part √∑ Percentage) √ó 100

Example: 25% of what number is 40?

Whole = (40 √∑ 25) √ó 100 = 1.6 √ó 100 = 160

Profit, Loss & Discount

Profit and Loss Formulas

Cost Price (CP): Price at which item is purchased

Selling Price (SP): Price at which item is sold

Profit = SP - CP (when SP > CP)

Loss = CP - SP (when CP > SP)

Profit % = (Profit/CP) √ó 100

Loss % = (Loss/CP) √ó 100

Profit Example

‡§®‡§´‡§æ ‡§â‡§¶‡§æ‡§π‡§∞‡§£

Loss Example

‡§§‡•ã‡§ü‡§æ ‡§â‡§¶‡§æ‡§π‡§∞‡§£

Simple & Compound Interest

Simple Interest (SI)

‡§∏‡§æ‡§ß‡•á ‡§µ‡•ç‡§Ø‡§æ‡§ú

SI = (P √ó R √ó T) / 100

P = Principal (initial amount)
R = Rate of interest per year
T = Time in years

Example:
P = ‚Çπ5000, R = 10%, T = 2 years
SI = (5000 √ó 10 √ó 2) / 100 = ‚Çπ1000
Amount = P + SI = ‚Çπ6000

Compound Interest (CI)

‡§ö‡§ï‡•ç‡§∞‡§µ‡§æ‡§¢ ‡§µ‡•ç‡§Ø‡§æ‡§ú

A = P(1 + R/100)·µÄ

CI = A - P

A = Final Amount
Interest compounds yearly

Example:
P = ‚Çπ1000, R = 10%, T = 2 years
A = 1000(1.1)¬≤ = ‚Çπ1210
CI = 1210 - 1000 = ‚Çπ210

‚òÖ Quick Tips & Facts

  • ‚òÖ 100% = the whole quantity (everything)
  • ‚òÖ 50% = half, 25% = quarter, 75% = three-quarters
  • ‚òÖ Percentages > 100% mean more than the original
  • ‚òÖ To find 10% quickly: divide by 10
  • ‚òÖ To find 1%: divide by 100
  • ‚òÖ Successive discounts are NOT additive!
  • ‚òÖ CI > SI when time > 1 year

Quick Calculation Tricks

Finding 15%: Find 10% + half of 10% (5%)

Finding 25%: Divide by 4

Finding 20%: Divide by 5

Finding 12.5%: Divide by 8

Successive % change: a% then b% = (a + b + ab/100)%

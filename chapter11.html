Introduction to Algebra

Algebra is the language of mathematics where we use letters and symbols to represent numbers and quantities. Instead of just working with specific numbers, algebra lets us work with general patterns and relationships.

Think of algebra as a powerful tool that helps us solve problems by creating equations and finding unknown values. Whether calculating distances, analyzing patterns, or solving real-world problems, algebra is essential.

This chapter introduces variables, constants, terms, coefficients, and expressions ‚Äî the building blocks of algebraic thinking, with 100+ practice problems to master the fundamentals.

Important Properties & Rules

Like Terms (‡§∏‡§Æ‡§æ‡§® ‡§™‡§¶)

Terms with the same variable(s) raised to the same power.

Examples: 3x and 5x are like terms | 2y¬≤ and 7y¬≤ are like terms

Can combine: 3x + 5x = 8x

Cannot combine: 3x and 5y (different variables)

Unlike Terms (‡§µ‡§ø‡§∑‡§Æ ‡§™‡§¶)

Terms with different variables or different powers.

Examples: 3x and 5y | 2x and 3x¬≤ | 4a and 7b

Rule: Unlike terms cannot be combined through addition/subtraction

Simplifying Expressions

Step 1: Identify like terms

Step 2: Combine like terms by adding/subtracting coefficients

Step 3: Keep unlike terms separate

Example: 5x + 3y - 2x + 7y = (5x - 2x) + (3y + 7y) = 3x + 10y

Substitution

Replace variables with given values and calculate.

Example: If x = 3, find 2x + 5

Solution: 2(3) + 5 = 6 + 5 = 11

‚òÖ Quick Tips & Key Points

  • ‚òÖ Variables are placeholders for unknown numbers
  • ‚òÖ Coefficient of x is 1, coefficient of -x is -1
  • ‚òÖ Only like terms can be added or subtracted
  • ‚òÖ Order doesn't matter: 2x + 3 = 3 + 2x (Commutative Property)
  • ‚òÖ Distributive Property: a(b + c) = ab + ac
  • ‚òÖ Expression ‚â† Equation (Expression has no = sign)

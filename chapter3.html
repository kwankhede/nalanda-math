Divisibility Tests

A Divisibility Test is a quick method to determine if one number divides another without performing full division.

These tests save time and make mental calculations much easier. Instead of dividing 12,345 by 3 the long way, you can simply add the digits (1+2+3+4+5 = 15) and check if 15 is divisible by 3!

This chapter covers divisibility rules for numbers 2 through 12, combination tests, real-world applications, and shortcuts ‚Äî with 1000+ practice problems to master these essential skills.

Divisibility by 7

7 ‡§®‡•á ‡§≠‡§æ‡§ó ‡§ú‡§æ‡§£‡•á

‚úì Example: 371 ‚Üí 37 - (1√ó2) = 35 ‚úì

Divisibility by 11

11 ‡§®‡•á ‡§≠‡§æ‡§ó ‡§ú‡§æ‡§£‡•á

Subtract sum of digits at odd positions from sum of digits at even positions. If result is 0 or divisible by 11, the number is divisible by 11.
‚úì Example: 121 ‚Üí (1+1) - 2 = 0 ‚úì
‚úì Example: 1,331 ‚Üí (1+3) - (3+1) = 0 ‚úì
‚úó Example: 123 ‚Üí (1+3) - 2 = 2 ‚úó

‚úì Example: 2,400 ‚Üí digits sum 6 ‚úì, last 2 digits 00 ‚úì
‚úó Example: 126 ‚Üí digits sum 9 ‚úì, but 26√∑4 ‚úó

Step-by-Step Examples | ‡§ö‡§∞‡§£-‡§¶‡§∞-‡§ö‡§∞‡§£ ‡§â‡§¶‡§æ‡§π‡§∞‡§£‡•á

Testing 12,345 for divisibility by 3:

Step 1: Add all digits ‚Üí 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 = 15
Step 2: Check if 15 √∑ 3 ‚Üí 15 √∑ 3 = 5 ‚úì
Result: 12,345 IS divisible by 3

Testing 7,896 for divisibility by 4:

Step 1: Take last 2 digits ‚Üí 96
Step 2: Check if 96 √∑ 4 ‚Üí 96 √∑ 4 = 24 ‚úì
Result: 7,896 IS divisible by 4

Testing 4,321 for divisibility by 11:

Step 1: Sum of odd position digits ‚Üí 4 + 2 = 6
Step 2: Sum of even position digits ‚Üí 3 + 1 = 4
Step 3: Subtract ‚Üí 6 - 4 = 2
Step 4: Is 2 divisible by 11? NO ‚úó
Result: 4,321 is NOT divisible by 11

‚òÖ Pro Tips & Tricks

  • ‚òÖ For 6, 12, 15, 18: Check component factors (e.g., 6 = 2√ó3)
  • ‚òÖ For large numbers with 3 or 9: Keep adding digit sums until you get a single digit

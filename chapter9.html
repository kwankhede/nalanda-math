BODMAS is a rule that tells us the correct order to perform mathematical operations in an expression.

Without BODMAS, 2 + 3 √ó 4 could equal 20 (if we add first) or 14 (if we multiply first). BODMAS ensures everyone gets the same correct answer: 14.

This chapter teaches you to solve complex expressions step-by-step using the BODMAS rule, with 1000+ practice problems to master the order of operations.

Understanding the Priority Order

1 Brackets ( ), { }, [ ] ‚Äî Always solve what's inside brackets first

2 Orders Powers (¬≤), Roots (‚àö) ‚Äî Then handle exponents and roots

3 Division & Multiplication √∑, √ó ‚Äî Equal priority, solve left to right

4 Addition & Subtraction +, ‚àí ‚Äî Equal priority, solve left to right

Priority Operation Symbol Example
1st Brackets ( ) { } [ ] (5 + 3) = 8
2nd Orders (Powers/Roots) ¬≤, ¬≥, ‚àö 3¬≤ = 9, ‚àö16 = 4
3rd Division & Multiplication √∑, √ó 12 √∑ 3 = 4, 4 √ó 5 = 20
4th Addition & Subtraction +, ‚àí 7 + 3 = 10, 9 ‚àí 2 = 7

Step-by-Step Examples

Example 1: Basic BODMAS

Solve: 5 + 3 √ó 4

Step 1: Identify operations We have Addition (+) and Multiplication (√ó)
Step 2: Follow BODMAS Multiplication comes before Addition
Step 3: Multiply first 5 + (3 √ó 4) = 5 + 12
Step 4: Then add 5 + 12 = 17

Example 2: With Brackets

Solve: (8 + 2) √ó 5 ‚àí 3

Step 1: Brackets first (8 + 2) √ó 5 ‚àí 3 = 10 √ó 5 ‚àí 3
Step 2: Multiplication 10 √ó 5 ‚àí 3 = 50 ‚àí 3
Step 3: Subtraction 50 ‚àí 3 = 47

Example 3: Complex Expression

Solve: 20 √∑ 4 + 3¬≤ √ó 2 ‚àí (5 + 1)

Step 1: Brackets 20 √∑ 4 + 3¬≤ √ó 2 ‚àí (5 + 1) = 20 √∑ 4 + 3¬≤ √ó 2 ‚àí 6
Step 2: Orders (Powers) 20 √∑ 4 + 3¬≤ √ó 2 ‚àí 6 = 20 √∑ 4 + 9 √ó 2 ‚àí 6
Step 3: Division & Multiplication (left to right) 20 √∑ 4 + 9 √ó 2 ‚àí 6 = 5 + 18 ‚àí 6
Step 4: Addition & Subtraction (left to right) 5 + 18 ‚àí 6 = 23 ‚àí 6 = 17

Types of Brackets

Bracket Priority (when nested)

1. ( ) ‚Äî Parentheses / Round brackets ‚Üí Solve these first

2. { } ‚Äî Braces / Curly brackets ‚Üí Then these

3. [ ] ‚Äî Square brackets ‚Üí Finally these

Example: 2 √ó [15 ‚àí {8 + (3 + 2)}]

Solve innermost first: (3 + 2) = 5
Then: {8 + 5} = 13
Then: [15 ‚àí 13] = 2
Finally: 2 √ó 2 = 4

Important Notes & Tips

‚ö†Ô∏è Division & Multiplication have EQUAL priority

When both appear, solve from left to right

Example: 12 √∑ 2 √ó 3 = 6 √ó 3 = 18 (NOT 12 √∑ 6 = 2)

‚ö†Ô∏è Addition & Subtraction have EQUAL priority

When both appear, solve from left to right

Example: 10 ‚àí 3 + 2 = 7 + 2 = 9 (NOT 10 ‚àí 5 = 5)

‚úì Alternative Names

BODMAS is also called:

‚Ä¢ BIDMAS (Indices instead of Orders)

‚Ä¢ PEMDAS (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication, Division, Addition, Subtraction)

All refer to the same rule!

‚ùå Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Solving left to right without BODMAS

5 + 3 √ó 4 = 8 √ó 4 = 32 ‚úó

5 + 3 √ó 4 = 5 + 12 = 17 ‚úì

Mistake 2: Not following left-to-right for equal priority

20 √∑ 4 √ó 5 = 20 √∑ 20 = 1 ‚úó

20 √∑ 4 √ó 5 = 5 √ó 5 = 25 ‚úì

Mistake 3: Ignoring brackets

2 √ó (3 + 4) = 2 √ó 3 + 4 = 10 ‚úó

2 √ó (3 + 4) = 2 √ó 7 = 14 ‚úì

Mistake 4: Wrong order for nested brackets

5 √ó [10 ‚àí (2 + 3)] = 5 √ó [10 ‚àí 2 + 3] ‚úó

5 √ó [10 ‚àí (2 + 3)] = 5 √ó [10 ‚àí 5] = 5 √ó 5 = 25 ‚úì

‚òÖ Quick Tips & Facts

  • ‚òÖ Always solve brackets first, working from innermost to outermost
  • ‚òÖ D and M have equal priority ‚Äî solve left to right
  • ‚òÖ A and S have equal priority ‚Äî solve left to right
  • ‚òÖ Use BODMAS in every expression with multiple operations
  • ‚òÖ Write each step clearly to avoid mistakes
  • ‚òÖ The order is: B ‚Üí O ‚Üí DM (left to right) ‚Üí AS (left to right)
  • ‚òÖ When in doubt, add extra brackets to make the order clear

HCF and LCM

HCF (Highest Common Factor) is the largest number that divides two or more numbers without leaving a remainder. Also called GCD (Greatest Common Divisor).

LCM (Lowest Common Multiple) is the smallest number that is a multiple of two or more numbers.

These concepts are fundamental for simplifying fractions, solving problems involving patterns, and working with ratios. This chapter covers multiple methods to find HCF and LCM, their relationship, and practical applications with 1000+ practice problems.

What is HCF?

The HCF of two or more numbers is the greatest number that divides all of them completely.

Example: HCF of 12 and 18

Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18

Common factors: 1, 2, 3, 6 ‚Üí HCF = 6

What is LCM?

The LCM of two or more numbers is the smallest number that is a multiple of all of them.

Example: LCM of 4 and 6

Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24...

Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 24, 30...

Common multiples: 12, 24, 36... ‚Üí LCM = 12

1. Addition of Integers | ‡§¨‡•á‡§∞‡•Ä‡§ú

  • Same signs: Add absolute values, keep the sign
    Example: (+5) + (+3) = +8, (-7) + (-2) = -9
  • Different signs: Subtract smaller absolute value from larger, take sign of larger
    Example: (+8) + (-3) = +5, (-10) + (+4) = -6
15 + (-8) = 7
(-12) + (-5) = -17
(-20) + 35 = 15

2. Subtraction of Integers | ‡§µ‡§ú‡§æ‡§¨‡§æ‡§ï‡•Ä

Change subtraction to addition of the opposite (additive inverse)

Rule: a - b = a + (-b)

7 - 10 = 7 + (-10) = -3
(-5) - 8 = (-5) + (-8) = -13
(-6) - (-9) = (-6) + 9 = 3
12 - (-5) = 12 + 5 = 17

  • (+) √ó (+) = (+) ‚Üí Positive √ó Positive = Positive

  • (+) √ó (-) = (-) ‚Üí Positive √ó Negative = Negative
  • (-) √ó (+) = (-) ‚Üí Negative √ó Positive = Negative

4. Division of Integers | ‡§≠‡§æ‡§ó‡§æ‡§ï‡§æ‡§∞

Same rules as multiplication for signs

  • (+) √∑ (+) = (+)
  • (-) √∑ (-) = (+)

‚òÖ Key Points to Remember | ‡§Æ‡§π‡§§‡•ç‡§§‡•ç‡§µ‡§æ‡§ö‡•á ‡§Æ‡•Å‡§¶‡•ç‡§¶‡•á

  • ‚òÖ The opposite (additive inverse) of +a is -a
  • ‚òÖ Every integer has a predecessor (n-1) and successor (n+1)
  • ‚òÖ Sum of an integer and its opposite is always 0
  • ‚òÖ Multiplying or dividing two integers with same sign gives positive result
  • ‚òÖ Multiplying or dividing two integers with different signs gives negative result
  • ‚òÖ On number line: greater numbers are always to the right

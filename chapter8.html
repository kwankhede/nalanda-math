1. Addition of Decimals | ‡§¨‡•á‡§∞‡•Ä‡§ú

Rule: Align the decimal points and add as you would with whole numbers.

  • Write numbers in columns with decimal points aligned
  • Add zeros if needed to make equal decimal places
  • Add from right to left, keeping decimal point in place
3.45
+ 2.8 (think: 2.80)
------
6.25

- 3.25

3. Multiplication of Decimals | ‡§ó‡•Å‡§£‡§æ‡§ï‡§æ‡§∞

Rule: Multiply as whole numbers, then place decimal point.

  • Ignore decimal points and multiply
  • Count total decimal places in both numbers
  • Place decimal point in answer from right
2.5 (1 decimal place)
√ó 3.4 (1 decimal place)
-----
8.50 (2 decimal places) = 8.5

4. Division of Decimals | ‡§≠‡§æ‡§ó‡§æ‡§ï‡§æ‡§∞

Rule: Make divisor a whole number by moving decimal point.

Example: 6.3 √∑ 0.9

  • Move decimal in divisor to make it whole: 0.9 ‚Üí 9
  • Move decimal same places in dividend: 6.3 ‚Üí 63
  • Now divide: 63 √∑ 9 = 7
6.3 √∑ 0.9 = 63 √∑ 9 = 7
12.5 √∑ 2.5 = 125 √∑ 25 = 5

‚òÖ Key Points to Remember | ‡§Æ‡§π‡§§‡•ç‡§§‡•ç‡§µ‡§æ‡§ö‡•á ‡§Æ‡•Å‡§¶‡•ç‡§¶‡•á

  • ‚òÖ The decimal point separates whole numbers from fractions
  • ‚òÖ Adding zeros after last decimal digit doesn't change value: 0.5 = 0.50 = 0.500
  • ‚òÖ Always align decimal points when adding or subtracting
  • ‚òÖ Count total decimal places when multiplying decimals
  • ‚òÖ To compare: 0.6 > 0.59 (because 0.60 > 0.59)
  • ‚òÖ Decimal to fraction: count decimal places as denominator (0.25 = 25/100)

CHAPTER 08

Decimals

दशांश

A decimal is a way of expressing parts of a whole using a decimal point (.). The digits after the decimal point represent fractions with denominators of 10, 100, 1000, and so on.

Decimals are everywhere in daily life: money (₹25.50), measurements (2.5 meters), weights (3.75 kg), temperatures (37.2°C), and scores (9.5/10). They provide a convenient way to express values between whole numbers.

This chapter covers decimal place values, reading and writing decimals, converting between fractions and decimals, comparing decimals, and performing all four operations—with 100+ practice problems to build mastery.

1. Decimal Notation

दशांश संकेत

The decimal point separates the whole number part from the fractional part.

Examples:
• 0.5 (five tenths = 5/10 = 1/2)
• 0.25 (twenty-five hundredths = 25/100 = 1/4)
• 3.75 (three and seventy-five hundredths)

2. Types of Decimals

दशांशाचे प्रकार

Terminating: Finite digits (0.5, 2.25)
Non-terminating: Infinite digits (0.333...)
Recurring: Repeating pattern (0.666...)

Note: We mainly work with terminating decimals in this chapter.

3. Reading Decimals

दशांश वाचणे

Read the whole part, say "point", then read each digit after decimal.

Examples:
• 12.5 → "twelve point five"
• 0.75 → "zero point seven five"
• 25.125 → "twenty-five point one two five"

4. Decimals & Fractions

दशांश आणि अपूर्णांक

Every decimal can be written as a fraction and vice versa.

Conversions:
• 0.5 = 5/10 = 1/2
• 0.75 = 75/100 = 3/4
• 3/4 = 0.75
• 1/2 = 0.5

5. Comparing Decimals

दशांशांची तुलना

Compare digits from left to right, starting after the decimal point.

Examples:
• 0.8 > 0.6
• 0.45 < 0.5 (0.50)
• 2.34 < 2.4 (2.40)
• 1.999 < 2.0

6. Decimals in Daily Life

दैनंदिन जीवनातील दशांश

Decimals are used everywhere around us.

Applications:
• Money: ₹45.50
• Length: 1.5 meters
• Weight: 2.75 kg
• Temperature: 36.5°C

Operations on Decimals | दशांशांवरील क्रिया

1. Addition of Decimals | बेरीज

Rule: Align the decimal points and add as you would with whole numbers.

  • Write numbers in columns with decimal points aligned
  • Add zeros if needed to make equal decimal places
  • Add from right to left, keeping decimal point in place
3.45
+ 2.8 (think: 2.80)
------
6.25

2. Subtraction of Decimals | वजाबाकी

Rule: Align decimal points, add zeros if needed, then subtract.

7.5 (think: 7.50)
- 3.25
------
4.25

Example 2:

10.0
- 6.38
------
3.62

3. Multiplication of Decimals | गुणाकार

Rule: Multiply as whole numbers, then place decimal point.

  • Ignore decimal points and multiply
  • Count total decimal places in both numbers
  • Place decimal point in answer from right
2.5 (1 decimal place)
× 3.4 (1 decimal place)
-----
8.50 (2 decimal places) = 8.5

4. Division of Decimals | भागाकार

Rule: Make divisor a whole number by moving decimal point.

Example: 6.3 ÷ 0.9

  • Move decimal in divisor to make it whole: 0.9 → 9
  • Move decimal same places in dividend: 6.3 → 63
  • Now divide: 63 ÷ 9 = 7
6.3 ÷ 0.9 = 63 ÷ 9 = 7
12.5 ÷ 2.5 = 125 ÷ 25 = 5

★ Key Points to Remember | महत्त्वाचे मुद्दे

  • ★ The decimal point separates whole numbers from fractions
  • ★ Adding zeros after last decimal digit doesn't change value: 0.5 = 0.50 = 0.500
  • ★ Always align decimal points when adding or subtracting
  • ★ Count total decimal places when multiplying decimals
  • ★ To compare: 0.6 > 0.59 (because 0.60 > 0.59)
  • ★ Decimal to fraction: count decimal places as denominator (0.25 = 25/100)
